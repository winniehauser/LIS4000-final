Children's Librarianship

Do you love children and reading? Do you feel joy in teaching children about the fun of reading and encouraging learning and exploration? Do you excel at doing fun voices when reading out loud? Do you want to see more cultures and people represented in books and media, and love to encourage diverse voices? If you answered yes to these questions, you may be interested in a career in Children's Librarianship!

Children's librarianship refers to librarians that specialize in Children's services and children's content. School librarians are similar, but not the same. (School librarians work in a school library, usually a media center). Those that specialize in children's services often have a background in childhood development and in some countries require additional librarian training. In the United States, there are over 60,000 children's librarians, with almost 90% of these being women (Children's Librarian Demographics and Statistics [2025]: Number of Children's Librarians in the US, 2021). Being a children's librarian is very rewarding, and involves creativity, compassion, exuberance, and passion for the job.

Interested in becoming a children's librarian? Read on to learn about the history of the field, one of the heroes of Children's Librarianship, latest news, and more. There are also important resources regarding Professional Organizations you can join, opportunities for professional development, and even some job listings to familiarize yourself with. Multiple journals and articles are linked throughout this LibGuide in order for you to dive even deeper in to Children's Librarianship and the work it encompasses. If you enjoy spending time with children, instruction, and having fun in your job every day, this could be the path for you.
