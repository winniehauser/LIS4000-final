Children's Librarianship

Contemporary State of the Field

The world can be a bleak place when you stop to look around at headlines and what's happening in your own community. As a children's librarian, you can make an impact, even in a small way, in making the world a better place and improving the lives of children. Helping a child learn to read and create a lifelong love for reading and learning is such a rewarding way to spend your career. With a career in children's librarianship, you could help many children with reading, set up programs for children to make both friends and crafts, create skills and knowledge, and create a safe community space in which to spend time and grow their brains. With cuts to funding, children's librarianship is more important than ever, as some school libraries are being cut, children's librarians at public libraries may have to step in and fill that role for young children. Additionally, the standards for school libraries (similar, but not the same as children's librarians in a public library) are changing (AASL Announces the Second Edition of the National School Library Standards for Learners, School Librarians, and School Libraries, 2025).
