Constructing Connections

Logistics of the Program

The library in this case will serve as the liaison point between these two groups, with the nonprofit acting as a catalyst for them. The students will be able to volunteer, and the unhoused individuals will get housing! It’s a win-win situation. Since many of the students at the Legacy Options high school will be construction certified, they will be able to build (or help build) the housing for the nonprofit (Colorado Village Collaborative). Students from the school and employees from CVC will meet at the library. Either we or the CVC folks will put on a presentation for the students, containing information about CVC, the people it serves, and the ways specifically that the students will being helping onsite. If students are working with construction, their specific projects and directions will be mentioned. If students are working with food serving or cleaning up of the sites and areas, their specific directions will too be mentioned. Some students who don’t have the construction background will be able to volunteer in alternate ways onsite at CVC if they so choose. If students and CVC need to have additional meetings, these can take place at the library. We or the CVC coordinators can sign volunteer verification papers if necessitated by the school.
