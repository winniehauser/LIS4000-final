About Colorado Village Collaborative

Colorado Village Collaborative exists to bridge the gap between the streets and stable housing by creating and operating transformational shelter communities in partnership with people experiencing homelessness.
  • Founded in 2017 with the Beloved Community Village (11 units)
  • First of its kind in Denver -> now 45 units in BCV & two other villages

    • The model
  • Staffing & services (24/7, security, 3 meals/day, case management)
  • Guidelines (quiet hours, community standards)
  • Site upkeep (sanitation, site walks, community clean-ups)
  • Accountability (trauma informed, conflict resolution, enforcement of community use agreements)
  • A common house provides a shared kitchen and restrooms with water and sewer services.
  • No time limits at the THV - people can stay as long as they need, with 70% eventually transitioning into stable housing and thriving
  • Effective in reducing homelessness, reducing emergency service utilization, fewer incarceration incidents, mental/behavioral management, and economic growth
